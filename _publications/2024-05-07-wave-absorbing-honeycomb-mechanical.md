## Abstract

Obtaining the mechanical properties of wave-absorbing honeycomb cell walls is the fundamental basis for investigating its cutting mechanism and optimizing machining parameters. Due to the manufacturing process of wave-absorbing honeycombs and the material characteristics of impregnated layers, the mechanical parameters calculated by conventional uniform-thickness cell models cannot accurately reflect the real mechanical performance of honeycomb panels.
This paper proposes a variable-thickness Y-shaped cell model. Based on the equivalent mechanical performance principle, an energy method is adopted to build the mapping relationship between cell wall mechanical parameters and the equivalent mechanical properties of wave-absorbing honeycomb structures. Image processing technology is utilized to extract structural parameters of the variable-thickness Y-shaped cell, and a 3D honeycomb model is constructed via periodic array of such cells.
Simulation results of out-of-plane elastic modulus and out-of-plane shear modulus of honeycomb cores show that the deviation between simulation and experimental data is controlled within 5%. Ultrasonic cutting simulations are carried out on honeycomb models built with variable-thickness Y-shaped cells and uniform-thickness cells respectively. Compared with experimental cutting force data, the cutting force error of the proposed variable-thickness model is only 13.67%, while the error of the traditional uniform-thickness model reaches 47.8%.
This research provides theoretical support and a novel analytical method for mechanical performance analysis, cutting mechanism research and parameter optimization of honeycomb composites with uneven wall thickness distribution.
